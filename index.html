My Journey Into Networking

My interest in networking came from challenging myself in an area I originally found difficult.

From avoiding networking to leading the networking portion of a capstone project

Earlier in my degree, networking was one of the subjects I struggled with and did not enjoy. Instead of continuing to avoid it, I volunteered to take ownership of the networking portion of my capstone project. That decision changed how I viewed the field. Through hands-on configuration, connectivity testing, and troubleshooting, I discovered that I enjoyed the investigative side of networking: finding where communication breaks, testing assumptions, and working through difficult problems until the root cause is found.

Featured Project

The project that best represents my technical growth and troubleshooting mindset.

Systems Administration & Network Infrastructure Capstone

Served as the networking lead for a multi-system capstone environment supporting a database-backed web application. The environment included Windows systems, Linux systems, a Kali Linux machine, LAN/WAN segmentation, and firewall infrastructure. My responsibility was the networking layer: configuring connectivity, supporting DNS/DHCP-related setup, testing communication, and troubleshooting end-to-end connectivity across the environment.

  • Led networking work for an environment with 3 Windows machines, 2 Linux machines, and 1 Kali Linux machine.
  • Worked with LAN, WAN, firewall, and segmented network communication concepts.
  • Performed terminal-based configuration and troubleshooting rather than relying on a graphical interface.
  • Tested system communication across the network and investigated connectivity failures.
  • Identified evidence that a virtual machine configuration issue was preventing proper communication.
  • Escalated the issue for review, which confirmed the misconfiguration and allowed the project environment to function properly.

What I Learned

This project taught me that strong troubleshooting requires more than trying random fixes. It requires testing assumptions, documenting what works and what fails, and having enough confidence in the evidence to keep investigating when the initial explanation does not make sense.

Why It Matters

The experience helped me develop a genuine interest in networking and infrastructure. It also showed me that I enjoy technical problems where the answer is not immediately obvious and where persistence, communication, and analysis are required.
